10 things we’ve learned after our first round of funding. (Nine months later)

We learned a lot more, but eleven would be too many

The product changes and so does your team

We have started off with a vision to change the world, now we are changing a specific well tested and analyzed part of the world. This affects how your team grows and what you all believe in. Change is good

It gets harder, not easier

Having an idea and building the POC is easy. The hard part is going full time, taking a pay cut, lawyers, contracts, time management, managing personal relationships, setting up the company, growing the company, believing you haven’t made a mistake doing this, etc. Good luck!

People are not always going to support you, but they still care

When you initially start looking for support you will reach out to family, friends and past colleagues. There will be some that will be there for you and others that won’t return your emails. You shouldn’t let this bother you because it’s not their fault they don’t understand or care about what you are doing. People will be there when they feel comfortable. Remember that when someone asks you to test their latest app.

Building a unicorn company does not happen overnight

Every day people are sold the start up dream of becoming a billionaire overnight. What you are not told is a lot of things have to go your way and your idea is not enough. I am not saying you shouldn’t chase your dreams, but nothing is given to you even if you are the chosen one. Be ready to work for every cent of that billion over a long period time.

Your team is everything

Without your team sticking together through the good, bad and scary times you will not succeed. One team, one dream

Your investors are everything too

If you raise a million and you have the best idea your investors could ruin vision. Choosing an investor is like choosing a life partner. Make sure you are both invested, for better or worse.

You learn more in this short period of time than you ever had before

We have hundreds of years of collective practical marketing, sales, engineering and business experience. We have needed every bit of it to work our way thought the wiggly bit that makes up creating a company like setting up payroll, hiring the right lawyers, understanding that people work differently, being a leader and making the right decisions all the time. If you don’t know, now you know.

Don’t recreate the wheel

It’s easy to think you have a better way of doing things than the people before you. These thoughts are shared with engineers, business and sales professionals. When you are building a company, it’s not your job to recreate the way these things are done. It’s your job to execute your vision not solve problems that have been solved before. Pick the right tool for the job and focus on what you are good at.

You will burn out

We have been working flat out the last nine months are planning to work even harder the next twelve months. You will burn out and be prepared for it to happen. It doesn’t mean you are not tough enough, dedicated or have enough passion. It means you are human. You are following your dream so take time to smell the roses. You will be better for it.

We would do it all over again

The moral of the story is we have come a long way and the journey is barely getting started. It's been a tough grind, but the self satisfaction of building something you dreamed up is an amazing feeling. Upward and onward!

Round two for us...
